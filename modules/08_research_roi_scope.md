# 08 Research ROI and Scope

## Purpose

Avoid spending months on low-return or infeasible directions.

## ROI Questions

For each idea, estimate cautiously:

- what data is needed;
- what compute/GPU time is needed;
- what implementation time is needed;
- what baselines must be reproduced;
- what risks may block results;
- what evidence is needed for the target venue or school;
- likely contribution level;
- whether the user has enough skill/time/resources.

## Kill / Pause Criteria

Pause or reject an idea when:

- the gap is likely already solved;
- required data is unavailable;
- baseline reproduction is unrealistic;
- expected contribution is too small;
- experiments cannot support the main claim;
- thesis requirements are unclear and deadline is near;
- engineering scope exceeds graduation timeline.

## Scope Ladder

Use four levels:

1. **MVP**: minimally working and demonstrable.
2. **Acceptable**: enough evidence for ordinary graduation or workshop-level report.
3. **Strong**: robust implementation, tests, analysis, clean writing.
4. **Research-upgrade**: novel angle, rigorous baselines, publishable story.

Do not push to Level 4 until Level 1-2 are safe for graduation projects.
